# Bright_TV_Viewership_Analysis

# Aim 

The Bright TV Project is a data-driven initiative aimed at supporting the company’s strategic goal of increasing its subscription base for the current financial year. As a digital television service provider, Bright TV seeks to better understand its users’ viewing behavior, engagement patterns, and demographic distribution in order to drive growth and improve customer retention.

# Expected Outcomes 

This project focuses on analyzing user data, including viewing duration, content preferences, geographic distribution, and audience demographics such as age and gender. By leveraging these insights, the project aims to identify key factors influencing content consumption and subscription trends.

The findings from this analysis will enable the Customer Value Management (CVM) team to:

- Develop targeted marketing strategies
- Optimize content offerings
- Improve user engagement and viewing experience
- Increase subscriber acquisition and retention rates

# Objectives 

- To measure and analyze Monthly Active Users (MAU) to understand overall platform usage
- To evaluate Average Viewing Duration to assess user engagement levels
- To identify Content Popularity by determining the most-watched channels and programs
- To assess the Engagement Rate to understand how frequently users interact with content
- To calculate the Churn Rate to identify the proportion of users leaving the platform
- To track the Subscriber Growth Rate to monitor increases in new subscriptions
- To examine Demographic Distribution (age, gender, and location) to better understand the target audience
- To determine the Retention Rate to evaluate how well the platform keeps its users over time

# Tools used in the project

Miro, Canva, Databricks and Excel 

# Key Insights

- Strong Growth Trend: Viewership increased by 26.17% (Jan–Mar), indicating rising platform engagement.
- Urban-Centric Audience: Majority of users are concentrated in Gauteng and Western Cape, showing a strong urban consumption pattern.
- Male-Dominated Viewership: ~87% of users are male, highlighting a significant gender imbalance and untapped female market.
- Core Audience = Working Age: ~74% of users are Young Adults & Adults, making them the primary target segment.
- Prime-Time Consumption: Over 70% of viewing occurs in afternoon–evening–night, with peaks on Fridays & Saturdays.
- Sports = Primary Driver: Live sports content dominates engagement and drives the longest viewing sessions.
- Low Engagement Depth: ~84% of users fall into low engagement tiers, indicating short viewing sessions and drop-offs.
- Content Behavior Varies by Demographics:
    Black audiences → high volume
    Indian/Asian audiences → deeper engagement
    Time-Driven Viewing Patterns: Consumption aligns with free time (after work/school & weekends) rather than random usage.
